How We Use Information

The limited, anonymized technical data that may be collected through third-party services (see Section 6) is used solely for the following purposes:

App Performance & Stability: Understanding crash rates and technical errors to improve reliability of the application.

Aggregate Usage Analytics: Understanding which features are most used in an entirely anonymous and aggregated way (e.g., total number of map opens per day — not tied to any individual).

Service Delivery: Map tile requests to OpenStreetMap servers require your IP address to receive map image data. This is a technical necessity of how web maps function.

Security: Protecting the App and its infrastructure from abuse, fraud, or unauthorized access through industry-standard technical measures.

⚠️
We do not use your data for: advertising, profiling, behavioral targeting, machine learning training on personal data, cross-app tracking, or any purpose beyond those listed above.

07

Cookies & Local Storage

The App itself does not set any cookies and does not use browser localStorage or sessionStorage to store personal data. All UI state (map position, active filters) is held only in JavaScript runtime memory and is lost when the App is closed or refreshed.

Third-party services (Google Fonts, OpenStreetMap, CDN providers) may set their own technical/functional cookies as part of standard web infrastructure operation. These are strictly necessary for content delivery and do not constitute behavioral tracking.

You can manage or delete cookies at any time through your browser or device settings. Disabling cookies will not impair the core functionality of Global Conflict Monitor.

08

Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organizational measures to protect the App and its infrastructure from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

HTTPS Encryption: All communications between the App and any server use TLS/HTTPS encryption, protecting data in transit from interception.

No Server-Side Storage: Because Global Conflict Monitor does not collect personal data, there is no personal data stored on our servers that could be compromised in a data breach.

Static Application: The App is served as a static HTML/CSS/JavaScript file with no backend database, server-side processing, or authentication endpoints — significantly reducing the attack surface.

Dependency Security: Third-party libraries are loaded from reputable CDNs (unpkg) and kept updated. We use Subresource Integrity (SRI) practices where possible.

While we strive for the highest security standards, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but commit to immediate disclosure of any breach affecting users.

GDPR — European Union Users

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or equivalent laws apply to you.

Legal Basis for Processing: Where any incidental technical data is processed (e.g., IP address for map tile delivery), the legal basis is legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) — specifically, the technical necessity of delivering map content over the internet. This processing is minimal, necessary, and proportionate.

Data Controller: Global Conflict Monitor Team acts as the Data Controller for any personal data processed in connection with the App.

International Data Transfers: Map tile requests may be processed by OpenStreetMap servers located in various countries. Google Fonts is operated by Google LLC (US). These transfers are covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where applicable.

Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you believe your data has been mishandled, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in the UK, CNIL in France, BfDI in Germany).

Data Protection Officer (DPO): For GDPR inquiries, contact us at dpo@conflictmonitor.app.

CCPA — California Residents

Categories of Personal Information Collected: In the past 12 months, we have not collected any personal information as defined under CCPA categories. Your California Rights: You have the right to know what personal information is collected, sold, or disclosed. You have the right to delete personal information. You have the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights. To exercise these rights, contact privacy@conflictmonitor.app with the subject line "CCPA Request".

Data Retention

No personal data is retained by us beyond the active session. All UI state held in browser/app memory is discarded when the App is closed.

Third-party services (OpenStreetMap, Google Fonts, CDN providers) retain standard server logs according to their own retention policies, which are typically 30–90 days for anonymized technical logs.

Deleting the App: Uninstalling the App from your device removes all locally stored App data. There is no account or cloud data to delete.

If we ever begin collecting data in the future (which would require an update to this policy and appropriate in-app disclosure), we will establish retention periods based on legal requirements and the minimum time necessary for the stated purpose.
